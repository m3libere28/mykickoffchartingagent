Chief Complaint: Pt referred by MD for MNT d/t recent stage 3a CKD diagnosis. Pt states "My doctor said my kidneys aren't working well and I need to change my diet."

PMH: CKD stage 3a (GFR 52), HTN, Gout. Meds: Losartan 50mg, Allopurinol 300mg, Amlodipine 5mg.

Weight History: CBW 210 lbs, Ht 5'10", BMI 30.1. Weight stable over past 6 months.

Nutrition Assessment:
- Pt reports high protein intake: eats red meat 5-6x/wk (8-12 oz portions), eggs daily, protein shakes post-gym.
- Estimated protein intake ~1.8g/kg/day (significantly exceeds CKD recommendations of 0.6-0.8g/kg).
- Sodium intake appears elevated: frequent use of table salt, canned soups, deli meats, processed snacks.
- Potassium intake moderate: eats bananas 1/day, tomato sauce 3x/wk, potatoes 2x/wk.
- Fluid intake: ~64 oz/day (adequate).
- No phosphorus binder prescribed at this time.
- Labs: GFR 52, BUN 28, Cr 1.6, K 4.8 (high normal), Phos 4.2, Na 140, Alb 3.8.

NFPE: Well-nourished appearance. No edema noted. Good muscle mass.

Counseling Provided:
- Educated pt on CKD dietary principles: importance of limiting protein, sodium, and monitoring potassium.
- Discussed target protein intake of 0.8g/kg/day (~76g/day based on ABW).
- Recommended reducing red meat to 2-3x/wk, portion sizes to 4-5 oz.
- Introduced plant-based protein options (beans, lentils, tofu) as partial replacements.
- Reviewed sodium reduction strategies: avoid adding table salt, read labels for <600mg sodium/serving.
- Discussed potassium management: moderate banana intake to 3x/wk, limit tomato sauce.

Plan:
- Goal: Reduce protein intake to ~80g/day within 6 weeks.
- Goal: Reduce sodium to <2000mg/day.
- Goal: Maintain potassium in normal range; monitor with labs.
- Goal: Discontinue protein shakes.
- Provided CKD-friendly meal plan sample and grocery list.
- F/u in 6 weeks with updated labs (BMP, Phos, GFR).
